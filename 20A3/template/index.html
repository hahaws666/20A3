Hello all people who are visiting this site!

The ongoing conflict in Ukraine is rooted in the annexation of Crimea by Russia in 2014, which was followed by pro-Russian separatist movements in eastern Ukraine. Since then, sporadic fighting and political instability have continued to cause widespread suffering for the people of Ukraine. The conflict has resulted in the displacement of over 1.5 million people, and thousands have lost their lives. Despite international efforts to mediate a ceasefire and find a diplomatic solution, the conflict has not yet been fully resolved. The situation remains tense, with occasional flare-ups of violence occurring in the region. The war has also had a significant economic impact on the country, exacerbating existing social and economic inequalities. The ongoing conflict in Ukraine is a reminder of the devastating impact that war and political instability can have on the lives of ordinary people. It is essential that the international community continues to support efforts to find a peaceful resolution to the conflict and provide humanitarian aid to those impacted by the crisis. It is also crucial to hold those responsible for the violence and violations of human rights accountable for their actions.

    ukraine situation

    Ukraine has seen intense fighting since 24 February 2022. This has led to a grave humanitarian crisis, with millions of people in dire need, including those who have fled across borders and many more who are on the move inside the country or unable to leave encircled towns and cities. The United Nations and partners have been assisting vulnerable communities in the conflict-torn eastern regions for the past eight years. Now we are collectively scaling up our supplies and services to meet the massive and urgent relief and protection needs for civilians across the country.
